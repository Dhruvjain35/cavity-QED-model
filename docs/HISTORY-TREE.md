## The lineage (six branches)

### Branch 1 — Foundations of light–matter coupling (quantum optics)
- **1954 — Dicke.** *Coherence in Spontaneous Radiation Processes.* Phys. Rev. **93**, 99. ✅
  Superradiance: N emitters radiate collectively → the seed of all "collective coupling" physics.
- **1958 — Hopfield.** *Theory of the Contribution of Excitons to the Complex Dielectric Constant of Crystals.* Phys. Rev. **112**, 1555. ✅
  **The polariton is born** — exciton + photon as one mixed quasiparticle. (Confirmed in evidence as the Hopfield-coefficient source.) *Root of the whole tree — the "1958" Shravan referenced.*
- **1963 — Jaynes & Cummings.** *Comparison of quantum and semiclassical radiation theories…* Proc. IEEE **51**, 89. ✅
  One atom + one mode, exactly solvable → the JC model (Spec §B.1).
- **1968 — Tavis & Cummings.** *Exact Solution for an N-Molecule–Radiation-Field Hamiltonian.* Phys. Rev. **170**, 379. ✅
  N emitters, one mode → the **√N collective coupling** (Spec §B.4–B.5) that the whole project hinges on.
- *(deeper root, context):* phonon-polariton ideas — Tolpygo (1950), Huang (1951). `[verify exact citations]`

### Branch 2 — Microcavity exciton-polaritons (condensed matter)
- **1992 — Weisbuch, Nishioka, Ishikawa, Arakawa.** *Observation of the coupled exciton-photon mode splitting in a semiconductor quantum microcavity.* Phys. Rev. Lett. **69**, 3314. ✅
  First semiconductor microcavity strong coupling → the iconic anticrossing (Spec §A).
- **1996 — Imamoğlu, Ram, Pau, Yamamoto.** *Nonequilibrium condensates and lasers without inversion: exciton-polariton lasers.* Phys. Rev. A **53**, 4250. `[verify]`
  Proposes the polariton laser.

### Branch 3 — Polariton condensation & quantum fluids of light
- **2006 — Kasprzak et al.** *Bose–Einstein condensation of exciton polaritons.* Nature **443**, 409. ✅
  Macroscopic coherence of polaritons — "BEC of light-matter."
- **2013 — Carusotto & Ciuti.** *Quantum fluids of light.* Rev. Mod. Phys. **85**, 299. ✅
  The field's standard review (our Spec §A dispersion equations come from here).
- **2017 — Lagoudakis & co. / "A polariton graph simulator."** New J. Phys. **19**, 125008. ✅ (the paper you handed me)
  Driven-dissipative condensate lattices map onto the **XY / Kuramoto model** → optical analog computing (Spec stretch view V9).

### Branch 4 — Polaritonic chemistry (the pivot to chemistry)
- **2012 — Hutchison, Schwartz, Genet, Devaux, Ebbesen.** *Modifying Chemical Landscapes by Coupling to Vacuum Fields.* Angew. Chem. Int. Ed. **51**, 1592. ✅
  **Polaritonic chemistry is born** — vacuum fields change a reaction.
- **2016 — Thomas et al. (Ebbesen group).** *Ground-State Chemical Reactivity under Vibrational Coupling to the Vacuum Electromagnetic Field.* Angew. Chem. Int. Ed. **55**, 11462. `[verify]`
  Vibrational strong coupling (VSC) alters *thermal* ground-state chemistry — the result that launched a thousand theory papers (and the controversy).

### Branch 5 — Theory machinery for molecular cavity QED
- **2015 — Galego, García-Vidal, Feist.** *Cavity-Induced Modifications of Molecular Structure in the Strong-Coupling Regime.* Phys. Rev. X **5**, 041022. `[verify]`
- **2019 — Campos-González-Ángulo, Ribeiro, Yuen-Zhou.** *Resonant catalysis of thermally activated chemical reactions with vibrational polaritons.* Nat. Commun. **10**, 4685. ✅ (Marcus–Levich–Jortner rate, Spec viz source)
- **2023 — Mandal, Taylor, Weight, Koessler, Li, Huo.** *Theoretical Advances in Polariton Chemistry and Molecular Cavity Quantum Electrodynamics.* Chem. Rev. **123**, 9786. ✅ DOI 10.1021/acs.chemrev.2c00855
  The big review (our Spec §B bright/dark-state equations come from here).

### Branch 6 — The controversy (open, unresolved — show it honestly)
- **2021 — Imperatore, Asbury, Giebink.** *Reproducibility of cavity-enhanced chemical reaction rates in the vibrational strong coupling regime.* J. Chem. Phys. **154**, 191103, DOI 10.1063/5.0046310. ✅
  Reports difficulty reproducing key VSC rate enhancements. *(Third author is Giebink, not Frank — corrected in deep-verify.)*
- **Open question (2021–now):** per-molecule coupling is ~10⁻³–10⁻² of a bond energy; how can a collective effect spread over 10⁶–10¹² molecules change *local* chemistry? The dark-state problem (Spec §B.6) remains the crux. **Settled:** strong coupling & polariton spectroscopy are real. **Unsettled:** whether/why VSC modifies thermal ground-state rates.

## Where this project sits (the leaf)
- **2024 — Sharma & Chen.** *Unraveling abnormal collective effects via the non-monotonic number dependence of electron transfer in confined electromagnetic fields.* J. Chem. Phys. **161**, 104102 (2024), DOI 10.1063/5.0225434. ✅ (preprint arXiv:2406.17101)
  Cavity-modified **electron transfer** via Holstein–Tavis–Cummings; predicts the **non-monotonic N turnover at N_max** (Spec §C, §6). The paper this whole collaboration is built on.
- **2025 — Yang et al.** *Designing strongly coupled polaritonic structures via statistical machine learning.* `[verify full ref]`
  ML for inverse-design of coupled structures (nearest ML prior art).
- **Multimode frontier (the genuine open antecedents):** Zhou, Chen, Sukharev, Subotnik & Nitzan, *Nature of polariton transport in a Fabry–Perot cavity*, Phys. Rev. A **109**, 033717 (2024), DOI 10.1103/PhysRevA.109.033717 (genuinely multimode — in-plane k continuum, ballistic polariton transport; the Chen-group multimode paper); and Ke & Assan, *Harnessing multi-mode optical structure for chemical reactivity*, J. Chem. Phys. **163**, 164703 (2025) (multimode rate enhancement single-mode theory misses). These — **not** arXiv:2511.04017, which is single-mode Ying & Nitzan — are the real multimode results the project points toward.
- **2026 — This project (Dhruv × Sharma).** Physics-informed Bayesian-optimization / GP surrogate for the HTC parameter space; this accurate single-mode simulation is the validated *oracle*, with the **multimode** extension as the open frontier. *(roadmap leaf — not yet a published result)*
